Jorge Luis Borges

24 August 1899 – 14 June 1986

Argentine short-story writer, essayist, poet, and translator. His work is known for its philosophical depth, intricate narratives, and explorations of time, infinity, and the nature of reality.

Notable works
Ficciones (1944)
El Aleph (1949)
Labyrinths (1962)

He became completely blind by his mid-fifties but continued to write and lecture. He described his blindness as a slow gift. It forced him to rely entirely on memory and imagination, which helped him build his fiction.

"I have always imagined that Paradise will be a kind of library."
